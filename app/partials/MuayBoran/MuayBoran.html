Developed on the battlefields of ancient Siam, Muay Thai Boran is a an umbrella term for a group of traditional Thai martial arts which seeks to train the practitioner to use his or her entire body as an attacking surface. When the ancient Thai warrior lost his weapons on the battlefield, he would resort to Muay Thai Boran to protect himself. In doing this, fundamental striking methods for the fist, open palm, forearm, elbow, shoulder, foot, shin, knee and hip are taught. Employing devastating striking, bone breaking joint locks and throws, this practical martial art is fast, efficient, resolute and deadly.

In addition to the offensive and defensive techniques of traditional Muay Thai Boran, specific stepping drills are practiced which include the 3 Treasures Step, Box Stepping and Star Stepping. The key advantage of training these varied stepping methods is the development of speed, balance and precision when advancing, retreating or turning the back.
